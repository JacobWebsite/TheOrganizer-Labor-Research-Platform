# ChatGPT Resources Report

**Subject:** Strategic Opportunities for a Labor Relations Research
Platform\
**Date:** March 2026

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Overview

This report summarizes strategic ideas and analytical opportunities for
a labor research platform built from integrated public datasets
including OSHA, WHD, NLRB, BLS, Census, SEC, IRS, and corporate
ownership data. The platform links employer records across these sources
to create a unified view of workplace conditions, labor relations, and
corporate structure.

The core opportunity is to transform disconnected government datasets
into a **labor intelligence system** capable of identifying organizing
opportunities, corporate labor risks, and structural patterns in the
U.S. labor market.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 1. Core Platform Concept

The most powerful version of this system is a **Labor Intelligence
Platform**.

Instead of simply storing data, the platform identifies patterns across:

-   workplace violations
-   union activity
-   corporate ownership
-   industry structure
-   labor market conditions

Key questions it could answer:

-   Which employers are most likely to experience union organizing?
-   Which corporate groups repeatedly violate labor laws?
-   Which industries are seeing worsening workplace conditions?
-   Where are organizing opportunities emerging geographically?

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Organizing Intelligence Tools

## Target Selection Engine

A scoring system could rank employers based on organizing viability.

### Key Signal Categories

**Worker Grievance Signals** - OSHA violations - Wage theft cases -
injury rates vs industry averages - benefits below industry norms

**Structural Feasibility** - industry union density - nearby unionized
employers - corporate network union presence

**Workforce Cohesion** - turnover rates - occupational concentration -
demographic workforce patterns

**Financial Capacity** - revenue per employee - PPP payroll counts -
federal contracts

**Organizing Momentum** - prior NLRB elections - unfair labor practice
filings - union mentions in web data

-   wage theft cases

# 3. Research and Journalism Applications

The combined datasets allow entirely new labor economics research.

### Example Research Questions

**Do workplace violations predict unionization?**

Regression models could test whether OSHA violations or wage theft cases
correlate with union election activity.

**Do private equity firms produce worse labor outcomes?**

Ownership data can be linked to enforcement records to measure violation
patterns across corporate networks.

**Do unions improve workplace safety?**

Comparing injury rates in union vs non-union establishments could
quantify union effects.

**Where are labor standards declining geographically?**

County-level analysis combining union density, violations, and wage data
could reveal regional labor market deterioration.

# 8. Ethical and Strategic Risks

### Employer Misuse

Companies could potentially monitor organizing signals.

Mitigation strategies include restricting sensitive real-time insights.

### Data Misinterpretation

Users may confuse correlation with causation.

Clear explanations and statistical notes should accompany analytical
outputs.

### Worker Privacy

The platform should avoid personal-level data and remain
employer-focused.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 9. Correlation vs Causation

Analyses should include statistical transparency.

Example:

Signal: OSHA Violations\
Correlation with union elections: positive

Interpretation:

Employers with higher violation rates have historically been more likely
to experience organizing campaigns, but violations alone do not cause
unionization.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 10. Strategic Vision

If developed successfully, the platform could become the **central
reference for labor relations in the United States**, similar to how
OpenSecrets became the primary resource for campaign finance research.

Key components of that vision include:

-   universal employer search
-   corporate ownership mapping
-   labor violation histories
-   union presence tracking
-   industry benchmarking
-   automated research summaries

The long-term outcome would be a comprehensive map of labor conditions
and workplace power across the American economy.
