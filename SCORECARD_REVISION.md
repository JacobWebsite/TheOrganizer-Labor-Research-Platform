# Scorecard Revision: The Strategic Targeting Engine
**Date:** February 26, 2026
**Status:** Strategic Roadmap

---

## 1. The Strategic Shift
The platform is transitioning from a **Reactive Database** (listing past violations) to a **Proactive Targeting Engine** (predicting future organizability). This requires moving from "Direct Data" (explicit filings) to "Inferred Data" and "Leading Indicators."

### The Three Pillars (Strategic Scorecard)
Every employer is now evaluated through three multidimensional lenses:
1.  **Anger (Motivation):** Blends safety/wage violations with real-time sentiment signals.
2.  **Stability (Winnability):** Prioritizes low turnover and fixed assets; stable workforces are required to build durable committees.
3.  **Leverage (Power):** Synthesizes corporate ownership, government contracts, financials (RPE), and political connections.

---

## 2. Solving the "Thin Profile" Problem
"Thin profiles" are employers who do not appear in government violation databases (OSHA/WHD). To solve this "Cold Start" problem, we use the following options:

### Data Option Catalog

| Option | Pillar | Signal Type | Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Digital Shadow** | Anger/Stability | Leading | Job posting velocity and review sentiment (Glassdoor/Indeed). |
| **2. Corporate Genealogy** | Leverage | Relational | Walking the ownership tree (GLEIF/SEC) to find "Hidden Union" connections. |
| **3. Demographic Inversion** | Winnability | Statistical | Using BLS Staffing Matrices + Census data to estimate workforce composition. |
| **4. Subsidies & Breaks** | Leverage | Tactical | Good Jobs First data to find state/local tax abatements and clawback levers. |
| **5. Regulatory Friction** | Anger | Leading | Local health ratings and environmental (EPA ECHO) permits. |
| **6. Political Exposure** | Leverage | Strategic | CEO campaign donations (FEC) to map the power structure. |
| **7. Supply Chain** | Leverage | Tactical | SEC "Major Customer" disclosures to find choke points (e.g., sole-source providers). |
| **8. Fixed Assets** | Stability | Structural | Property tax records to identify companies that cannot easily relocate. |

---

## 3. Implementation Roadmap

### Phase A: Statistical Inversion (High ROI, Low Data Cost)
*   **Demographic Inversion:** Synthesize BLS Industry Staffing Patterns with local Census Tract data.
*   **Revenue-per-Employee (RPE):** Use 2022 Economic Census ratios to estimate workforce size for "invisible" employers.

### Phase B: Power Mapping (Data Integration)
*   **Genealogy:** Implement recursive SQL queries to "leak" union signals from parents to subsidiaries.
*   **Subsidies:** Match Good Jobs First records to the canonical employer index.

### Phase C: Digital Shadow (Web Intelligence)
*   **Turnover Pulse:** Track hiring spikes via job board APIs/scrapers as a proxy for workforce churn.
*   **Political Mapping:** Connect donor records to the corporate crosswalk.

### Phase D: Strategic Choke Points (Complex Modeling)
*   **Supply Chain Extraction:** Use LLMs to parse SEC "Risk Factors" for customer dependencies.
*   **Asset Lock:** Pilot a property-tax-to-employer matching system in a major metropolitan area (e.g., NYC).

---

## 4. Next Steps
The immediate priority is **Phase A**, specifically the **Demographic Inversion** and **RPE Estimation**. These models utilize data already available within the platform to immediately enrich all 146,000 employer profiles, making the "thin profiles" searchable and strategically actionable.
