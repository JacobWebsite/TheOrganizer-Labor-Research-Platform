# Labor Relations Research Platform — Strategic Vision & Brainstorming Report

**Date:** March 4, 2026
**Subject:** Transforming Multi-Source Labor Data into a Predictive Intelligence Engine

---

## Executive Summary: From Reactive Lookup to Strategic Intelligence

The unification of over a dozen U.S. government sources into a single, deduplicated **Master Employer ID** infrastructure represents a paradigm shift in labor research. By connecting the "Anger" (OSHA/WHD violations), "Aspiration" (BLS wage gaps), and "Leverage" (Corporate hierarchy/Federal contracts) pillars, this platform moves from a simple database to a **Labor Intelligence Engine**.

The following report outlines how this data can be operationalized for organizers, researchers, workers, and policymakers.

---

## 1. For Union Organizers: The "Strategic Targeting Engine"

The most valuable tool for an organizer is not a search bar; it is a **Ranked Opportunity Feed**.

### A. The "Heat Map" of Unrest
Instead of searching for a specific company, organizers should see a list of non-union employers in their jurisdiction ranked by a "Propensity to Organize" score.
*   **Key Signals:** High OSHA violations (Grievance) + Low wages relative to QCEW/OES industry averages (Economic incentive) + Recent PPP loans or high Revenue-per-Employee (Ability to pay) + Proximity to a successful NLRB win in the same sector (Momentum).

### B. The "Vulnerability Dossier"
A one-page automated briefing for a specific employer that maps their "Pressure Points."
*   **The Narrative:** "This employer is a federal contractor (SAM.gov) with $10M at stake, but they have 5 pending wage theft cases (WHD). They are owned by a Private Equity firm (GLEIF) that is currently facing a strike at a different subsidiary (F7 cross-reference)."
*   **Actionable Insight:** Identifies exactly where the employer is weak (e.g., "Mention the $200k in backwages at the first gate meeting").

### C. The "Lookalike" Finder
"Find me employers exactly like the one we just unionized in Scranton."
*   **Strategy:** Utilize the **Similarity Scoring** (Gower distance) to find employers with the same NAICS, similar employee counts (Form 5500), and similar demographic makeup (ACS) who are currently non-union.

## 2. For Researchers & Journalists: The "Inequality Investigator"

This platform can reveal systemic "Labor Malpractice" that is currently invisible to the public.

*   **The "Safety-Union Gap" Index:** A journalist could generate a report showing: "In the warehousing industry, non-union shops have 40% higher injury rates (OSHA) than union shops (F7) in the same zip code, despite similar sizes (CBP)."
*   **The "Federal Subsidy vs. Labor Violation" Tracker:** An investigative story waiting to be told: "The top 100 federal contractors in [State] have collectively paid $50M in wage theft penalties while receiving $2B in taxpayer funds."
*   **The "Corporate Family" Audit:** Identifying "Bad Actor" parents. "Parent Company X has a 12% violation rate across its unionized subsidiaries but a 45% violation rate across its non-union ones." This proves the "Union Advantage" with hard data.

---

## 3. For Workers: The "Know Your Worth" Portal

Workers engage with data when it directly impacts their livelihoods and safety.

*   **The "Employer Report Card":** A simple, mobile-friendly search where a worker types their company name and sees:
    *   **Grade A-F on Safety:** Based on OSHA violations vs. industry peers (SOII).
    *   **The "Wage Gap":** "You make $18/hr. The unionized average for your job in this city is $24/hr (OES + F7)."
    *   **The "Ownership Transparency":** "Your boss isn't just 'Bob'; your company is owned by a billionaire investment group in NYC (CorpWatch)."
*   **The "Comparison Tool":** "Show me what the union contract (CBA) looks like at the hospital across the street." Linking the `cba_provisions` to the worker's current employer profile to show exactly what they are missing.

---

## 4. For Policymakers: The "Early Warning System"

*   **Sectoral Distress Signals:** If a specific industry (e.g., Nursing Homes in NJ) shows a simultaneous spike in JOLTS turnover, OSHA violations, and WHD cases, the platform flags this as a "Sector in Crisis" for legislative intervention.
*   **Taxpayer Accountability:** A dashboard for state AGs to see which companies receiving state tax breaks (matched via EIN) are currently under investigation for wage theft or NLRB unfair labor practices.

---

## 5. Strategic Platform Design & Future Roadmap

### The "Killer App": Unified Corporate Crosswalk
The ability to say "This OSHA record, this 990 tax filing, and this Union contract are all the same entity" is the "holy grail" of labor research. Maintaining the **Master Employer ID** registry is the platform's primary value proposition.

### AI-Powered Intelligence
*   **The "Dossier Architect":** Use LLMs (like Gemini) to read 50+ OSHA violation descriptions and summarize them into a human-readable narrative: *"They have a recurring problem with forklift safety and have ignored three warnings since 2022."*
*   **Natural Language Query:** Allowing an organizer to ask: *"Find me the biggest non-union hospitals in the South with more than 500 employees and at least one safety violation in the last year."*

### Missing Data & Growth
*   **WARN Act Notices:** Layoff data is a critical signal for both organizing and policy.
*   **State-level Enforcement:** Adding state-specific OSHA/WHD programs to close the "Federal-only" gap.
*   **Real Estate/Property Data:** Identifying if an employer owns or leases their facility to assess their mobility and vulnerability to pressure.

The platform succeeds when a veteran organizer, who has relied on "gut feelings" for decades, looks at a dossier and says: *"I didn't know they were owned by that private equity firm—we have a contract with their other company in Ohio. I'm calling that Local right now."* 

That connection is the bridge between data and power.
