# V8.5 Architecture Analysis -- Results Summary

**Date:** 2026-03-11
**Purpose:** Determine whether a per-category expert selection architecture
can beat V8's single-expert gate system, and characterize the model's
fundamental limits.

---

## What V8.5 Tested

Four experiments, each building on the previous:

1. **Per-dimension ceiling analysis** -- What's the theoretical best if we
   could pick the perfect expert per race category per company?
2. **Expert G solo evaluation** -- Expert G was identified as best for
   Black/Hispanic. How does it perform on all 1,000 holdout companies?
3. **Two+ clamp + D+G blend** -- G has a +13.33pp Two+ bias. Fix it, then
   blend D (for White/Asian) with G (for Black).
4. **Hybrid D + occ-chain adjustment** -- Instead of using G as a model,
   extract G's occupation-chain signal and use it to adjust Expert D.

---

## Results

### Experiment 1: Per-Dimension Ceiling

| Approach | Race MAE (pre-cal) |
|----------|-------------------|
| Single best expert per company | 3.357 |
| Per-category oracle (best expert per race cat) | **2.387 (-28.9%)** |
| Average top 2 experts | 3.457 (worse) |
| Average ALL experts | 4.994 (much worse) |

**Finding:** 28.9% improvement is theoretically possible but requires knowing
ground truth. Averaging experts always hurts -- you must SELECT, not blend.

**87% dimension disagreement:** For 87% of companies, the best expert for race
is different from the best for Hispanic or gender. The 1-expert-for-everything
architecture leaves massive performance on the table in theory.

### Experiment 2: Expert G Solo

| Expert | Race MAE | Black MAE | P>20pp | P>30pp | Two+ bias |
|--------|---------|-----------|--------|--------|-----------|
| D | 4.856 | 8.873 | 19.9% | 8.6% | +2.27 |
| **G** | **7.240** | **8.519** | **41.8%** | **18.7%** | **+13.33** |
| V6 | 4.911 | 8.927 | 20.5% | 8.6% | +2.36 |

**Finding:** Expert G is globally best for Black (8.52 vs D's 8.87) but
catastrophically bad overall. Root cause: `Two+` is a garbage residual
(`100% - White - Black - Asian - AIAN`). The occ_chain table doesn't track
NHOPI or Two+ separately, so any under-counting of main categories inflates
Two+. Worst individual errors: -83pp (Black), -79pp (Black).

### Experiment 3: Two+ Clamp + D+G Blend

| Scenario | Race MAE | Black MAE | P>20pp |
|----------|---------|-----------|--------|
| D solo | 4.856 | 8.873 | 19.9% |
| D+G raw blend | **4.809** | **8.582** | **18.6%** |
| D+G clamped | 4.865 | 8.860 | 19.7% |

**Finding:** Clamping Two+ made things WORSE (-0.057pp). The raw blend was
better because G's Two+ overflow accidentally pulled probability mass away
from White -- which D over-predicts for diverse companies. Clamping removed
this accidental benefit without replacing it with anything.

### Experiment 4: Hybrid D + Occ-Chain Signal

Tested adjusting D's estimates toward occ_chain values at varying weights.

**All-category adjustment (adjust White, Black, Asian, AIAN):**
- Best weight: 0.00 (no adjustment). Every weight made Race MAE worse.
- Occ_chain White directional accuracy: 42% (worse than coin flip).

**Black-only adjustment:**
- Best weight: 0.50 (Race MAE 4.808 vs 4.856 baseline).
- Black MAE improved: 8.610 vs 8.873.
- Occ_chain Black directional accuracy: 59.8% (barely above random).

**Per-sector at weight=0.15:**
- South: -0.041 (helps)
- **Healthcare: +0.113 (hurts!)**
- Admin/Staffing: -0.064 (helps)
- Construction: -0.059 (helps)

**Finding:** The occupation signal is too coarse (naics_group x state) to
reliably correct D. "Healthcare in Florida" covers both a nursing home in
the Panhandle and a hospital in Miami. 60% directional accuracy for Black
means 40% of the time the adjustment goes the wrong way.

## Conclusions

### What We Proved

1. **The 1-expert gate architecture is theoretically suboptimal** (87% of
   companies have different best experts for different dimensions), but the
   practical alternatives we tested don't capture this theoretical gain.

2. **Expert G's occupation-chain signal contains real information** (best
   for Black globally) but is contaminated by a broken Two+ residual and
   too coarse geographically (state-level).

3. **Simple averaging and blending always hurt.** You must know WHICH expert
   to use WHERE, and our ability to predict that is limited (~60% accuracy).

4. **~4.5pp Race MAE (post-calibration) is near the floor** for census-based
   estimation. The remaining errors are structural: companies whose workforces
   diverge from geographic census data in ways that no publicly available
   data source captures at the establishment level.

### What Didn't Work

| Approach | Why It Failed |
|----------|--------------|
| D+G per-category blend | G isn't actually better for Black on all companies, just on average |
| Two+ clamp | Removed an accidental benefit of Two+ overflow |
| Occ-chain as D adjustment | Only 60% directionally correct; too coarse geographically |
| All-experts average | Noise from weak experts overwhelms signal |

### What's Left for V9

The productive V9 directions are NOT about expert architecture:

1. **Fix Expert G's Two+ computation** in `occ_local_demographics` table to
   properly track NHOPI and Two+ from source data, then re-evaluate
2. **More granular calibration** -- the V8 regional calibration was right
   in concept but county_tier:high had no data. More training data or
   looser thresholds (N>=20?) could help
3. **Non-census data sources** -- employer-reported data, LinkedIn workforce
   signals, H-1B filings, CMS provider staffing data for Healthcare
4. **Segment-specific honesty** -- extend YELLOW confidence floor to all
   companies where the model knows it's unreliable (not just NAICS 56/72/23)
